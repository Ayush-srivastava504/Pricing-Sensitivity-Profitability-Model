# Pricing Sensitivity & Retail Profitability Model

### *Revenue is Vanity. Profit is Sanity.*

---

## Executive Overview

This project investigates a retail business generating **$2.0M+ in gross revenue** but experiencing stagnant profit growth.

Through transaction-level analysis across **10,000+ records**, the objective was to uncover hidden profit leaks and identify structural inefficiencies within unit economics.

The analysis revealed that **18% of potential profit was being eroded** due to inefficient shipping distances and elevated product return rates.

**Target Audience:** Founders, CFOs, and Senior Operations Leaders seeking data-driven operational clarity.

---

## Business Objective

**Primary Goal:**
Identify and quantify “Profit Leaks” across operational workflows and pricing structures.

**Core Questions Addressed:**

* Where is margin erosion occurring?
* Which cost centers are destroying profitability?
* At what point does discounting or shipping distance become value-destructive?
* Which categories provide sustainable margin resilience?

## Strategic Insights

### 1. Financial Bridge Analysis (Waterfall)

**Finding:** Logistics and refunds were the largest contributors to margin erosion.
While marketing was assumed to be the primary issue, shipping costs consumed a greater share of profitability.

---

### 2. Logistics Efficiency Threshold

**Finding:** Furniture shipped beyond 1,200 KM becomes non-profitable.
Distance acts as a structural margin destroyer.

Every incremental kilometer past this threshold compounds value destruction.

---

### 3. Returns & Double Shipping Effect

**Finding:** Bookshelves exhibited a 15% higher return rate than category average.

High return rates trigger:

* Refund cost
* Reverse logistics
* Replacement shipping

This “double shipping” dynamic materially reduces contribution margin.

---

### 4. Pricing Sensitivity Simulation

A dynamic discount model revealed:

* Profit turns negative beyond the sustainable discount threshold.
* Categories respond differently to pricing strategies.
* Margin resilience varies significantly across product lines.

This enables strategic discount planning rather than reactive promotions.
---

### 5. Pricing Sensitivity Simulation

   **Q: What happens if we start discounting our products?**
    A: Profit declines steadily, and beyond the sustainable threshold, the business turns unprofitable.

---

   **Q: What is the company’s current margin?**
   A: 22% baseline profit margin.

---

   **Q: How much discount can the company safely offer?**
   A: Only up to the breakeven discount level. Beyond that point, profit becomes negative.

---

   **Q: Are all categories equally sensitive to discounts?**
   A: No. Some categories maintain healthier margins under discount pressure, while others experience faster margin erosion.

---

   **Q: Why is this important?**
   A: Instead of blindly running promotions, leadership can simulate pricing strategies before execution and avoid structural margin collapse.

## Actionable Recommendations

1. **Dynamic Shipping Pricing**
   Introduce a distance-based surcharge beyond 1,200 KM.

2. **Returnless Refund Strategy**
   For low-ticket items (< $20), eliminate reverse logistics costs.

3. **Marketing Budget Reallocation**
   Shift 10% of spend from low-margin Furniture to higher-margin Electronics.

4. **Discount Governance Policy**
   Establish a maximum sustainable discount cap to prevent structural margin collapse.
